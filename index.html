THINGS I'VE DONE THAT I WOULD DO AGAIN

Hello, World! My name is Jessica and here I'm going to talk about some cool things I've done in the past 5 years that were really fun and that I would do again if I have the opportunity. These recent days are not being easy to digest because of all of the reasons we already know. So one of the things I like to do is to remember all of the fun I had when everything was so-called "normal", and plan for more adventures!

SINGING AND DANCING ON THE STREETS

Actually, this one wasn't my idea. I was too shy and scared to sing and dance in the middle of the streets. When I was overseas I joined this evangelism group honestly thinking I could make some friends there because they were all almost the same age as me. But I really didn't know what was coming! I had to rehearse plays and perform wearing funny clothes and having my face painted black and white, I had to learn songs to sing in the street in broad daylight, I had to learn choreographies (dancing steps) and perform in the middle of the busiest streets of the cities we went. This was all very scary but also really fun, because first, I wasn't the only one getting embarrassed; second, we were traveling to different countries together (the advantage of being in Europe where things like that are much easier). In the end I had a priceless experience and I miss all that excitement and adrenaline!

LIVE IN ANOTHER COUNTRY AND LEARN THE LANGUAGE

I remember it as if it was yesterday, I woke up that day determined to go to another country to learn English and to have an adventure. So in the middle of 2016 I went to Ireland, alone, my English knowledge was close to zero, and I stayed there for 1yr and 4 months. The best 16 months of my life. Today I like to think that my life was one before that trip, and another after that. Having the experience to leave your confort zone, being "forced" to communicate in another language, learning the ways of the people is something that I wouldn't trade for anything! I want to repeat it! The whole crazy experience. I'm still living in another country (in the US now) and my English is still not perfect and I don't really think it will ever be, but I think it's good enough so I can jump to another language in the near future. I wish I could do the same, go to the country and live it all. I just can't decide where would be the next language and destination... France, Italy, Spain, Germany, Russia???

TRAVEL WITH FRIENDS

What is even better than traveling by yourself? Traveling with friends! I once spent 10 awesome days traveling between various destinations with two friends. That was so fun! I know that alone you can go only to the places you are really interested in, and you can do things at your own pace. But it's so good to have people to share these moments with, to laugh together, to face the unknown, to have mistakes and successes, to try good and bad foods.. And there's always some crazy stuff that you can only do "safelly" if you have your friends around. We are already dreaming for the next time the world will need to deal with us together again.
